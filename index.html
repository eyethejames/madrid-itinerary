Introduction

Madrid today is a city layered with monuments, plazas, and institutions whose origins stretch deep into the Early Modern period. During the rise of the Spanish Empire, the capital became the central stage where royal power, imperial ambitions, and cultural exchange were continuously negotiated and displayed. Many of the spaces that visitors walk through today — whether grand squares, religious sites, botanical gardens, or aristocratic palaces - carry the physical traces of this global history.

This interactive map introduces three thematic pathways that make these connections visible. Theme A explores the foundations of imperial authority and the enduring symbols that shaped Spanish identity. Theme B highlights the material culture and elite memory that commemorated imperial power in everyday urban life. Theme C follows the networks of knowledge, science, and global exchange that animated Madrid as an intellectual capital of a vast empire.

Together, these nine locations create a guided journey into how the Early Modern Empire was remembered, represented, and lived within the urban fabric of Madrid. This project invites the viewer to navigate the city as both a physical and historical landscape, seeing how imperial memory remains embedded in the spaces that continue to define Madrid today.

Conclusion

This tour offers just a glimpse into the many ways empire is embedded in Madrid’s architecture and public memory. The sites across the three themes show how political authority, cultural expression, and scientific knowledge were intertwined in shaping both the city and the Spanish Empire. We hope this map helps you see familiar landmarks through a new interpretive lens — and encourages further exploration of how history continues to shape the spaces we inhabit today.

Exploring Madrid through the lens of empire reveals a city where memory is constantly present — sometimes monumental and explicit, other times quiet and woven into the everyday rhythm of urban life. These nine locations remind us that imperial history is not preserved only in archives or textbooks, but in the very spaces people inhabit, visit, and move through.

Each theme offers a different way of reading the city: how royal authority was staged, how elites curated their past, and how global knowledge shaped the intellectual life of the capital. Taken together, they form a narrative of Madrid as a city shaped by its role at the centre of a vast, interconnected world.

This map encourages visitors to continue looking beyond the surface of the city — to observe how places carry stories, how monuments shape identity, and how the legacies of empire continue to inform Madrid’s character today.
